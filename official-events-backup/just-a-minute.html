Event Overview

Just A Minute (JAM) is an exciting speech competition that challenges participants' quick thinking, verbal fluency, and mental agility. Contestants are given an impromptu topic and must speak on it for exactly one minute without hesitation, repetition, or deviation.

This fast-paced event tests your ability to think on your feet and articulate your thoughts clearly under pressure. It's a thrilling challenge of verbal dexterity that pushes participants to maintain coherence while carefully monitoring their speech patterns and time.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Time Limit: Exactly 1 minute.
  • No Hesitation: Participants must not pause or show undue hesitation while speaking.
  • No Repetition: Words or phrases should not be unnecessarily repeated (except for connecting words).
  • No Deviation: Speech must remain relevant to the assigned topic.
  • Topics: Will be provided on the spot, with 30 seconds of preparation time.
  • Challenges: Other participants can challenge if they spot a rule violation. Valid challenges earn points.
  • Scoring: Based on speaking time, valid challenges, and overall fluency.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Tips for Participants

  • Practice speaking continuously on random topics to build fluency.
  • Vary your sentence structure to avoid repetition.
  • Have a mental structure ready: introduction, main points, and conclusion.
  • Learn to recognize when you're about to hesitate and quickly change your approach.
  • Listen carefully to other participants to spot valid challenges.
