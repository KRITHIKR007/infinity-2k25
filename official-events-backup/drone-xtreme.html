DroneXtreme

Aerial Agility & Precision Challenge

Venue

IIAME Drone Arena

Team Size

2-3 members

Registration Fee

₹600 per team

Event Description

DroneXtreme is the ultimate test of drone piloting skills, challenging teams to navigate custom-built quadcopters through intricate obstacle courses and precision challenges. This event combines technical knowledge, piloting expertise, and strategic thinking.

Participants will face multiple challenges designed to test different aspects of drone flight, from high-speed racing to precision maneuvers. This event is designed for both experienced pilots and newcomers wanting to experience the thrill of competitive drone flying.

Drone Specifications

Team-Provided Drones

Size & Weight

  • Frame size: 250mm diagonal or smaller
  • Maximum takeoff weight: 500g
  • Propeller size: 5 inches maximum

Power System

  • Battery: 3S or 4S LiPo only
  • Maximum voltage: 16.8V
  • Motors: 2300KV maximum

Electronics

  • Flight controller: Any F4 or lower
  • Radio system: 2.4GHz only
  • Failsafe mechanisms required

Camera System

  • FPV camera required
  • Video transmitter: 25mW maximum
  • 5.8GHz frequency band only

Organizer-Provided Drones

Teams who don't have their own drones can use the event-provided drones for a small additional fee. These are standard racing drones with the following specifications:

  • 220mm frame with durable carbon fiber construction
  • F4 flight controller with Betaflight firmware
  • 1500mAh 4S LiPo batteries (multiple provided)
  • FPV system with goggles and controller provided
  • Pre-tuned and tested for optimal performance

Note: Teams using organizer-provided drones must pay a security deposit of ₹5,000, refundable upon returning the drone in proper condition.

Competition Format

1

Qualification Round: Time Trial

All teams will navigate a standard course with gates, flags, and obstacles. Teams get three attempts, with the best time counting. The top 16 teams advance to the competition phase.

2

Phase 1: Speed Circuits

Head-to-head knockout races on a high-speed circuit. Pilots must complete 3 laps, with the faster pilot advancing to the next round. Missing gates incurs a 5-second penalty.

3

Phase 2: Precision Challenge

Pilots navigate through small hoops and perform precision maneuvers like hovering and controlled descents. Points are awarded for successful completion of each element within time limits.

4

Final Challenge: Ultimate Obstacle Course

The top 4 teams compete on an advanced obstacle course combining speed sections, precision elements, and technical challenges. Scores are based on completion time and accuracy.

Rules & Regulations

  • Safety First: All drones must have prop guards and be equipped with a failsafe system. Any flights deemed unsafe by judges will result in disqualification.
  • Flight Area: Drones must remain within the designated flight area at all times. Flying outside boundaries results in disqualification.
  • Crash Recovery: If a drone crashes, the pilot has 30 seconds to recover and continue or forfeit the current run.
  • Interference: Any attempt to jam or interfere with other teams' radio signals will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Repairs: Teams may repair drones between rounds only in designated areas. A maximum of 10 minutes is allowed for repairs.
  • Inspections: All drones will be inspected before each phase to ensure they meet specifications and safety requirements.
