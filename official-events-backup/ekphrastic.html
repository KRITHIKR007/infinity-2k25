Poetry Writing Competition

Ekphrastic

A poetry competition where participants create a poem based on an artwork given at the event.

Register Now Back to Events

Event Overview

Ekphrastic is a unique poetry competition that explores the relationship between visual art and written word. Participants will receive an artwork at the beginning of the event and will be challenged to create a poem inspired by what they see, interpreting emotions, narratives, and symbolism present in the image.

This event tests both creative writing skills and the ability to analyze visual media, translating visual sensations into poetic expression. It celebrates the intersection of different art forms and the unique perspective each poet brings to their interpretation.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Artwork Provision: Participants will receive an artwork at the start of the event.
  • Poem Length: Maximum 20 lines.
  • Time Limit: 45 minutes to complete the poem.
  • Format: Poems can be in any style or form (free verse, sonnet, haiku, etc.).
  • Language: Poems must be written in English.
  • Judging Criteria: Creativity, interpretation of the artwork, emotional impact, and language proficiency.
  • Plagiarism: All submissions must be original. Plagiarism will result in disqualification.
  • Participants must bring their own writing materials (pen, pencil, etc.).
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Tips for Participants

  • Take time to observe the artwork carefully, noting details, colors, composition, and the emotions it evokes.
  • Consider both what is explicitly shown in the artwork and what might be implied.
  • Use specific imagery and sensory details that connect directly to elements in the artwork.
  • Focus on crafting original metaphors and similes that expand on the visual elements.
  • Remember that an ekphrastic poem doesn't just describe the artwork—it responds to it, creating a dialogue between visual and written art.
