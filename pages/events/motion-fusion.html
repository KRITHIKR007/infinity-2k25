Motion Fusion

A specialized videography competition focusing on motion, transitions, and storytelling through dynamic visuals.

Register Now Back to Events

Event Overview

Motion Fusion is a creative videography competition that challenges participants to excel in the art of visual storytelling through dynamic motion and compelling transitions. This event invites filmmakers to showcase their technical expertise and artistic vision in producing short video compositions that capture the essence of movement.

Participants will have the opportunity to demonstrate their skills in videography, editing, and creative direction. Whether it's through dance choreography, urban landscapes, nature's movements, or abstract concepts - this competition rewards innovative approaches to capturing and expressing motion through the lens.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Theme: Movement and Transitions
  • Individual or Team: Participants can enter individually or in teams of up to 3 members
  • Duration: Submitted videos must be 1-3 minutes in length
  • Format: MP4 or MOV format, minimum resolution of 1080p
  • Equipment: Participants can use any filming equipment (DSLR, mirrorless, smartphone, drone, etc.)
  • Content: All content must be original work created by the participant(s)
  • Music/Audio: Copyright-free or properly licensed music must be used
  • Submission: Entries must be submitted three days before the event via the provided online platform
  • Judging Criteria:
  • Technical quality (25%)
  • Creative use of transitions and motion (30%)
  • Storytelling and concept (25%)
  • Audio integration (10%)
  • Overall impact (10%)
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding

Tips for Participants

  • Plan your transitions carefully - they should enhance your story, not distract from it
  • Experiment with different frame rates to capture motion in creative ways
  • Consider how audio and visual elements work together to create a cohesive experience
  • Focus on a specific concept or theme to give your video a clear direction
  • Pay attention to pacing - alternating between fast and slow movements can create dynamic contrast
