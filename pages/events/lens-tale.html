Event Description

Lens Tale is a photography competition that challenges participants to capture compelling visual narratives through their camera lenses. This event celebrates the art of photography as a powerful medium of storytelling, creative expression, and documentation.

Whether you're an amateur enthusiast or a seasoned photographer, Lens Tale invites you to showcase your perspective, technical skills, and artistic vision. Participants will compete across multiple categories, each exploring different dimensions of photographic expression.

Competition Categories

Nature & Landscape

Capturing the beauty and diversity of natural environments, landscapes, flora, fauna, weather phenomena, and natural formations. Submissions should showcase the photographer's ability to capture the essence and magnificence of the natural world.

Urban Life

Documenting city life, architecture, street scenes, and the rhythm of urban environments. This category emphasizes the human element within built environments, exploring the relationship between people and the spaces they create and inhabit.

Portrait & Human Emotion

Focusing on capturing the essence of individuals or groups, revealing character, emotion, and human stories. Entries can range from formal portraits to candid shots that convey authentic moments and emotional depth.

Abstract & Conceptual

Exploring creative, experimental, and conceptual approaches to photography. This category celebrates innovative compositions, unique perspectives, creative techniques, and photographs that convey ideas, themes, or emotions through non-literal representation.

Campus Chronicles (Special Category)

Capturing the essence and spirit of campus life during Infinity 2025. This special category challenges participants to document the vibrant atmosphere, memorable moments, and unique character of the festival through their lenses.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Submission Requirements: Each participant may submit up to 3 photographs across all categories (maximum 2 per category).
  • Originality: All photographs must be original works taken by the participant within the last 12 months. No previously published or awarded photos are eligible.
  • Technical Specifications:
    • Format: JPEG or PNG
    • Resolution: Minimum 2000 pixels on the longest side
    • File size: Maximum 10MB per image
  • Editing Guidelines: Basic editing (cropping, color correction, contrast adjustment) is permitted. However, excessive manipulation that alters the authenticity of the photograph is not allowed. Composite images, HDR, and focus stacking are acceptable only in the Abstract & Conceptual category.
  • Ethics: Photographs must adhere to ethical standards. Images depicting harm to subjects or environments, or those taken by trespassing or violating privacy laws will be disqualified.
  • Copyright: Participants retain copyright of their images. By submitting, participants grant Infinity 2025 non-exclusive rights to use submitted photographs for promotional purposes with appropriate credit.
  • Caption & Description: Each photograph must include a title and a brief description (max 100 words) explaining the context, story, or concept behind the image.
  • Equipment: Photographs taken with any device (DSLR, mirrorless camera, smartphone, etc.) are eligible. Technical quality appropriate to the equipment used will be considered during judging.

Submission Process

  1. Register for Lens Tale through the Infinity 2025 website.
  2. After registration, you will receive a confirmation email with a link to the submission portal.
  3. Upload your photographs along with titles and descriptions through the portal.
  4. Ensure all submission requirements are met before the deadline.
  5. For the "Campus Chronicles" category, photographs must be taken during Infinity 2025 event days and submitted by 6:00 PM on the final day of the festival.

Judging Criteria

Technical Quality (25%) - Exposure, focus, composition, and technical proficiency

Creativity (25%) - Originality, unique perspective, and innovative approach

Visual Impact (20%) - Immediate emotional response and memorable quality

Storytelling (20%) - Narrative quality and ability to convey meaning

Relevance to Category (10%) - Alignment with the theme of the chosen category
