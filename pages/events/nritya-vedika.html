Event Description

Nritya Vedika is a vibrant group dance competition that celebrates the power of synchronized movement and creative choreography. Teams are invited to present captivating performances that showcase cultural richness, technical prowess, and artistic vision.

From classical Indian dance forms to contemporary fusion, hip hop, folk, or any dance style of your choice, this platform welcomes diversity and innovation. Bring your team's unique energy and storytelling abilities to create an unforgettable performance.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Team Composition: Each team must consist of 5-15 members. Cross-college teams are allowed.
  • Time Limit: Each performance must be between 6-8 minutes. Exceeding the time limit will result in point deductions.
  • Dance Styles: Teams can choose any dance style or combination of styles - classical, folk, contemporary, hip-hop, Bollywood, fusion, etc.
  • Music Submission: Teams must submit their final audio track in MP3 format at least two days before the event. Name your file with your college name and team name.
  • Props & Effects: Props are allowed but must be arranged by the team. Use of fire, water, or dangerous props is strictly prohibited. All props must be set up and removed within 1 minute before and after the performance.
  • Costumes: Teams are responsible for their own costumes and makeup. Costumes should be appropriate and aligned with the theme of the performance.
  • Reporting Time: All teams must report at the venue at least 2 hours before their scheduled performance time for technical rehearsals and stage orientation.
  • Content Guidelines: Performances must be suitable for all audiences. Any vulgarity or offensive content will lead to immediate disqualification.

Judging Criteria

25% Choreography

  • Originality and creativity
  • Complexity of movements
  • Use of stage space
  • Transitions between sequences

25% Synchronization

  • Coordination among team members
  • Timing with music
  • Formation changes
  • Group cohesion

20% Technical Execution

  • Precision of movements
  • Dance technique
  • Energy level and stamina
  • Difficulty level of routine

15% Expression & Theme

  • Emotional connection
  • Storytelling elements
  • Interpretation of music
  • Theme development

15% Overall Presentation

  • Costumes and appearance
  • Use of props (if any)
  • Stage presence
  • Audience engagement

Competition Format

1

Preliminary Round

All registered teams will perform their complete routine. The judges will select the top 8 teams based on the judging criteria to advance to the final round.

2

Final Round

The shortlisted teams will perform once more on the main stage. Teams may enhance or modify their performances from the preliminary round. Winners will be selected based on their final performance.
