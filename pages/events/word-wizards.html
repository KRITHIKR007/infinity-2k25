Event Description

Word Wizards is a comprehensive literary competition that celebrates the power and beauty of language across multiple formats. This event brings together wordsmiths of all kinds - poets, storytellers, debaters, and eloquent speakers - to showcase their linguistic prowess and creative expression.

From crafting compelling narratives to delivering persuasive arguments, Word Wizards offers diverse platforms for participants to demonstrate their mastery over words. Whether you wield your pen with precision or your voice with vigor, this competition invites you to showcase your literary talents.

Competition Categories

Poetry Slam

A dynamic spoken word competition where participants perform original poetry that blends elements of performance, writing, competition, and audience participation. Poets will be judged on content, delivery, and audience impact.

Time Limit: 3-5 minutes per performance

Flash Fiction Challenge

Participants will craft compelling short stories within strict word limits, based on provided prompts or themes. This category tests concise storytelling ability and creative imagination in a time-constrained environment.

Word Limit: 500 words maximum

Oxford-Style Debate

A formal debate format where participants argue for or against given motions. Debaters will be evaluated on logical reasoning, persuasive speaking, rebuttal skills, and overall argument structure.

Format: 1v1 debates with opening statements, rebuttals, and closing arguments

Impromptu Speaking

Test your quick thinking and oratory skills as you deliver speeches with minimal preparation. Participants will receive random topics and have just 60 seconds to prepare before presenting a coherent and engaging speech.

Time Limit: 2-3 minutes per speech

Rules & Guidelines

General Rules

  • Participants may enter up to two different categories.
  • All work must be original and created by the participant.
  • Plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Content should be appropriate for a diverse audience (no explicit content).
  • Time limits will be strictly enforced with signals at 30 seconds remaining.

Category-Specific Rules

Poetry Slam:

  • No props, costumes, or musical instruments allowed.
  • Poems must be memorized, not read from paper.
  • Scoring based on content (50%) and performance (50%).

Flash Fiction:

  • Stories must incorporate provided prompts or themes.
  • Word count between 300-500 words.
  • Submissions must be typed in 12-point Times New Roman, double-spaced.

Debate:

  • Positions (for/against) will be assigned randomly.
  • Personal attacks on opponents are prohibited.
  • Interruptions during opposing speeches are not allowed.

Impromptu Speaking:

  • Only 60 seconds of preparation time allowed.
  • No reference materials permitted during preparation.
  • Speeches must relate directly to the assigned topic.
