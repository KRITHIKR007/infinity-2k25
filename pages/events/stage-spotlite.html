Event Description

Stage Spotlite is a theatrical competition that celebrates the art of storytelling through drama, comedy, mime, and monologues. This platform invites teams to showcase their acting prowess, directorial vision, and creative storytelling abilities on stage.

From powerful socially relevant dramas to entertaining comedies, innovative mime performances to compelling monologues, Stage Spotlite offers participants the opportunity to captivate audiences and bring stories to life through theatrical expression.

Performance Categories

One-Act Play

Teams present a complete theatrical piece with character development, plot, and resolution within a 15-20 minute timeframe. Plays can be original works or adaptations of existing scripts (with proper attribution).

Team Size: 4-12 members including actors, director, and backstage crew

Comedy Sketch

Short humorous performances that can range from situational comedy to satire, parody, or stand-up. These pieces focus on entertainment value while demonstrating clever writing and comedic timing.

Team Size: 4-8 members including performers and writers

Mime & Physical Theater

Performances that rely entirely on body movement, facial expressions, and gestures without spoken words. These pieces must convey a clear narrative or concept through physical expression alone.

Team Size: 4-6 performers

Dramatic Monologue

Solo performances where a single actor presents a compelling character through an extended speech or internal dialogue. These performances showcase acting depth, emotional range, and character embodiment.

Team Size: 1 performer plus 1-3 support members (director, writer, etc.)

Rules & Guidelines

General Rules

  • Time Limits: One-Act Plays (15-20 minutes), Comedy Sketches (8-12 minutes), Mime Performances (6-10 minutes), and Monologues (4-6 minutes). A 5-minute setup and 3-minute strike time will be provided.
  • Original Content: All scripts should preferably be original. If adapting existing works, proper attribution must be given, and copyright considerations must be addressed.
  • Language: Performances can be in English or Hindi. For regional language performances, a brief synopsis in English must be provided.
  • Content Guidelines: Content should be appropriate for a general audience. Excessive profanity, explicit content, or hateful speech will result in disqualification.
  • Technical Requirements: Basic lighting and sound support will be provided. Technical requirements must be submitted 5 days before the event.
  • Props & Costumes: Teams are responsible for bringing their own props and costumes. Minimal set pieces are allowed. Set design should be simple and quick to assemble.
  • Script Submission: A copy of the script/performance outline must be submitted 1 week before the event for content review.

Category-Specific Rules

One-Act Play:

  • Must have a clear beginning, middle, and end structure
  • At least 3 characters must have substantial roles
  • Set changes during performance must be executed smoothly by cast members

Comedy Sketch:

  • Can be a series of related sketches or one continuous piece
  • Must maintain comedic intent throughout the performance
  • Improvisational elements are allowed if noted in advance

Mime & Physical Theater:

  • No spoken words allowed, but sound effects or music permitted
  • Must tell a coherent story through movement alone
  • Minimal props allowed; focus should be on physical expression

Dramatic Monologue:

  • Must be performed from memory, no reading from script
  • Should demonstrate character development and emotional range
  • Minimal movement/blocking recommended; focus on delivery
