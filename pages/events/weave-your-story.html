Historical Speech Competition

Weave Your Story

A historical speech competition where participants recreate and deliver famous speeches from history.

Register Now Back to Events

Event Overview

Weave Your Story is a unique historical speech competition that brings the greatest orators of history back to life. Participants will select, memorize, and perform a famous historical speech, embodying the character, emotion, and historical context of the original delivery.

This event challenges participants to research historical figures, understand the social and political climate of the time, and deliver speeches with conviction and authenticity. It's an opportunity to connect with history's most powerful rhetoric and bring those words to a contemporary audience.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Speech Selection: Participants must choose a historically significant speech delivered before 2000.
  • Time Limit: 3-5 minutes per performance.
  • Authenticity: Participants should research and try to recreate the original delivery style.
  • Memorization: Speeches should be memorized, not read.
  • Context: Before the speech, participants must provide a brief (30-second) introduction explaining the historical context.
  • Judging Criteria: Historical accuracy, delivery, emotional impact, memorization, and character portrayal.
  • Props/Costume: Simple props or costume elements are allowed but not required.
  • Participants must submit their chosen speech for review one week before the event.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Tips for Participants

  • Research not just the speech but also video/audio recordings if available to understand the original delivery style.
  • Practice in front of a mirror or record yourself to refine your delivery and gestures.
  • Study the historical context thoroughly to understand the speech's significance and emotional undertones.
  • Consider the cadence, rhythm, and emphasis patterns of the original speech.
  • Try to embody the historical figure's persona while maintaining clarity for a modern audience.
