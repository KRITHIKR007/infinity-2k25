Event Description

Model Blitz is a fast-paced SolidWorks design competition that challenges participants to create detailed 3D models within tight time constraints. This event tests your CAD skills, design thinking, and ability to work under pressure.

Whether you're a SolidWorks beginner or an experienced designer, this competition offers a platform to showcase your modeling abilities and creative problem-solving skills through multiple rounds of increasing complexity.

Competition Format

1

Round 1: Basic Modeling (45 minutes)

Participants will be given engineering drawings of mechanical components to model in SolidWorks. This round tests fundamental modeling techniques and accuracy.

Part Modeling Dimensioning Drawing Interpretation
2

Round 2: Assembly Challenge (60 minutes)

The top 50% of participants from Round 1 advance to create assemblies from provided and self-created components. This round evaluates assembly techniques and mate constraints.

Assembly Modeling Mate Constraints Part Modifications
3

Round 3: Design Innovation (90 minutes)

The top 10 participants will be given a real-world engineering problem to solve through both design and documentation. This final round tests creativity, engineering principles, and presentation skills.

Advanced Modeling Engineering Analysis Technical Documentation Design Optimization

Rules & Requirements

  • Software: Participants must be familiar with SolidWorks 2022 or later. The competition will use SolidWorks 2023 installed on lab computers.
  • Prerequisites: Basic knowledge of part modeling, assemblies, and engineering drawings is expected.
  • Resources: No external resources (including internet access, design libraries, or personal storage devices) are allowed during the competition.
  • Submission: All models must be saved in the provided competition folder with specified naming conventions.
  • Timing: Each round has a strict time limit. Models submitted after time expires will receive point deductions.

Judging Criteria

Modeling Accuracy (30%) - Precision of dimensions and features

Modeling Technique (30%) - Appropriate use of features and methods

Completion (20%) - Amount of the required model completed

Efficiency (10%) - Speed and optimal use of features

Innovation (10%) - Creative solutions and design choices
