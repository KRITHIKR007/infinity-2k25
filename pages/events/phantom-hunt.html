Event Description

Phantom Hunt is an immersive cybersecurity challenge that puts your skills to the test in a simulated environment. Teams will navigate through a series of increasingly complex challenges, from cracking encrypted messages to analyzing network traffic and uncovering digital footprints.

This multi-phase competition tests your knowledge of cybersecurity concepts, digital forensics, and ethical hacking principles. Can your team track down the phantom hacker before time runs out?

Competition Structure

1

Phase 1: Digital Breadcrumbs

Teams solve CTF-style challenges using QR codes, encrypted messages, and OSINT techniques to gather intelligence about the phantom hacker. Each clue leads to the next, requiring careful analysis and creative thinking.

2

Phase 2: Network Infiltration

Teams analyze intercepted packets, trace malicious activities, and bypass security measures in a controlled environment. This phase tests technical skills and problem-solving abilities under pressure.

3

Phase 3: Final Showdown

The top-performing teams advance to the final challenge, where they face advanced cybersecurity puzzles against the clock. This phase combines all previously tested skills into a comprehensive security challenge.

Rules & Regulations

  • Team Composition: 4-5 members per team with at least one member having basic networking and cybersecurity knowledge.
  • Allowed Tools: Wireshark, Cisco Packet Tracer, basic encryption/decryption tools, and OSINT resources. All tools must be approved by the organizers before use.
  • Prohibited Actions: Sharing answers with other teams, attacking the competition infrastructure, or using unauthorized external resources.
  • Time Limit: Each phase has a specific time limit. Teams must submit their solutions before time expires.
  • Equipment: Teams should bring their own laptops with required software pre-installed. Power outlets and internet will be provided.
  • Ethics: All activities must follow ethical hacking principles. Any attempt at actual hacking or unauthorized access will result in immediate disqualification.
